How a Digital Signature Works

It's not a picture of your signature. It's a secure cryptographic process that binds your identity to a document.

1. The Signing Process

A
Hash the Document: A unique fingerprint of the document is created.
B
Encrypt the Hash: This fingerprint is encrypted using the signer's secret Private Key.
C
Bundle & Send: The encrypted hash (the signature) is attached to the original document.

2. The Verification Process

A
Recipient Creates New Hash: A new fingerprint is calculated from the received document.
B
Decrypt the Signature: The signer's Public Key is used to decrypt the original fingerprint.
C
Compare: If the two fingerprints match, the signature is valid!
